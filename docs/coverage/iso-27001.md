## Boundary disclaimer

HUMMBL is **not** an ISO 27001 certification body. Certification requires an accredited registrar (e.g., BSI, Lloyd's, DNV) conducting Stage 1 + Stage 2 audits per ISO/IEC 17021. This matrix maps HUMMBL technical primitives to Annex A controls. ISO 27001 certification additionally requires:
- An Information Security Management System (ISMS) — organizational system, not a software product
- Documented context, scope, leadership commitment, planning, support, operation, performance evaluation, improvement (Clauses 4–10) — organizational artifacts
- Risk assessment + risk treatment per Annex A — organizational process informed by HUMMBL evidence

HUMMBL evidence artifacts support both the ISMS Clause 7.5 (Documented information) and Annex A control implementation; HUMMBL does not constitute the ISMS itself.

## ISMS Clauses 4–10 — narrative

The ISO 27001 ISMS structure (Clauses 4–10) is organizational by design: it requires the organization to define its context (4), demonstrate leadership commitment (5), plan ISMS objectives and risk treatment (6), provide resources and competence (7), operate the planned controls (8), evaluate performance (9), and continually improve (10). HUMMBL contributes to:

- **Clause 7.5 (Documented information)** — append-only governance bus + cognition ledger satisfy "control of documented information" requirements (creation, identification, format control, distribution, access, retrieval, retention, disposition with audit-trail integrity)
- **Clause 8 (Operation)** — `INTENT` + adverse-event + risk-treatment tuples = operational evidence; risk-register integration per the Krineia connector spec (`founder_mode/docs/design/krineia/CONNECTOR_RISK_REGISTER_SPEC.md`) where a structured register is required
- **Clause 10 (Improvement)** — nonconformity tuple + corrective-action tracking + signed audit log

The ISMS itself — context, scope, leadership, planning — is the customer organization's responsibility.
