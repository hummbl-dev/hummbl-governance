## Boundary disclaimer

HUMMBL is not an IEEE 3119 conformity-assessment body and does not certify procurement processes. IEEE 3119-2025 is a voluntary consensus standard (soft law) published by the IEEE SSIT/SC committee. It targets procurement professionals acquiring commercial AI products and services (CAIPS) via formal contract frameworks; it does not cover in-house AI development or hybrid public-private development. The standard defines high-risk AI systems as those that make or are a substantial factor in making consequential decisions with significant impacts on people, groups, or society. Procurement-process execution, vendor selection, and contract authorship are the customer organization's responsibility. HUMMBL maps technical primitives to the standard's risk-management, evidence-collection, monitoring, and governance-maturity requirements.

## Scope summary

IEEE 3119-2025 establishes a uniform process model for procuring AI/ADS across six core processes: Problem Definition (Clause 6), Solicitation Preparation (Clause 7), Vendor Evaluation (Clause 8), Solution Evaluation (Clause 9), Contract Negotiation (Clause 10), and Contract Monitoring (Clause 11). The standard augments customary procurement lifecycle activities with AI-specific risk management methodologies — identifying, analyzing, evaluating, prioritizing, mitigating, and controlling unique AIS risks. It includes 26+ tools and rubrics (e.g., risk-appetite analysis, vendor evaluation scoring guide, AI procurement risk register). The standard aligns with EU AI Act Chapter III, Colorado AI Act, Texas HB 1709, and complements ISO/IEC 42001:2023.
