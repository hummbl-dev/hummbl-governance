## Boundary disclaimer

HUMMBL is **not** an IEEE 7001 conformance assessment body. IEEE 7001-2021 is a process standard defining measurable, testable levels of transparency (0–5) for autonomous systems across five stakeholder groups. Conformance is determined by demonstrating that transparency properties are present — not by certification from IEEE. HUMMBL maps technical primitives to the standard's transparency-level requirements and its two processes (System Transparency Assessment [STA] and System Transparency Specification [STS]); selecting target levels, conducting ethical risk assessments, and producing user-facing documentation are the customer-organization's responsibility.

## Scope summary

IEEE 7001-2021 is broadly applicable to all autonomous systems, including both physical (e.g., autonomous vehicles, care robots) and non-physical (e.g., medical diagnosis systems, chatbots) systems. Safety-critical systems and systems with the capacity to cause physical, psychological, societal, economic, environmental, or reputational harm are within scope. Systems using machine learning and their training datasets are within scope when considering system-wide transparency. System-to-system transparency (transparency of one system to another) is out of scope; transparency of the engineering process and of subsystem interactions is in scope. The standard defines five stakeholder groups — end users, the wider public and bystanders, safety certification agencies, incident/accident investigators, and lawyers/expert witnesses — each with normative transparency-level definitions from 0 (none) to 5 (highest). Levels are cumulative (incremental) for certification agencies, investigators, and legal experts; non-cumulative for users and the public.
